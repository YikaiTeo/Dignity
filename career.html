Youth Vocational Skills & Entrepreneurship Program Lead

Dignity is searching for a passionate program developer to build and pioneer Dignity’s Shop School concept together with us – bringing together practical skills education with business learning. We are searching for a passionate educator with program developing experience to join our team.

This individual will strategically develop and lead Dignity’s Skills Training and Education Program (STEP) to deliver our vocational courses to 200 secondary youth aged 16-18 years old each year.

Key Responsibilities: click to see more
Program and People Management
  1. Develop, monitor and evaluate work, performance and staff development plans
  2. Engage with STEP trainers and industry specific partners to assist with curriculum design, update and review
  3. Work closely with Dignity’s Secondary Education department to ensure the STEP program is aligned with the education program
  4. Identify opportunities for partnerships/collaborations and maintain a network of external partners that can supplement the STEP program or provide internships to students
Program Development
  1. Develop an innovative, learner-centered environment for student learning in each skills class
  2. Develop and open new vocational skills classes based on identified student needs
  3. Plans for projected skills classes and modifies existing skills classes
Program Monitoring
  1. Oversee implementation of STEP Program activities and ensure that program quality is upheld, is consistent with Dignity’s principles and pedagogical approach
  2. Develop and implement strategy to ensure the delivery of all program requirements are completed and reported upon accurately
  3. Assess instructional effectiveness of trainers and program for review and improvement
  4. Ensure budgets for each program/class is completed and viable
Requirements: click to see more
  1. Degree holder (Education, psychology or business an asset)
  2. Experience in training and teaching; program development & monitoring
  3. Strong leadership and team management capabilities
  4. Strong verbal and interpersonal skills, with ability to communicate cross-culturally and relate to diverse groups of individuals
  5. Proactive, organised, detail-oriented
  6. Computer proficient

Case Worker

Our teams try our very best to redeem the childhoods of society’s most vulnerable members, the children of refugees and Malaysia’s own urban poor.

Children need to see love in action. They also need a safe space to learn to be resilient, not to see themselves as victims of circumstances, and to be equipped to face all that life will throw at them.

Key Responsibilities: click to see more
  1. Conduct pre-screening interviews for child survivors and vulnerable individuals based on best interest procedure standards
  2. Ensure accurate data entry into the database system for all welfare cases
  3. Perform quality control checks to finalise cases for case conferences and supervision meetings
  4. Provide accurate and relevant information to supervisor, stakeholders and focal points
  5. Communicate with immediate supervisor regarding developments in daily caseloads, including urgent protection needs or medical conditions of applicants and their families
  6. Assist with advocacy and community development programs for marginalised communities
  7. Provide timely reports on situation at the grass root level by identifying pathological trends by conducting community profiling
  8. Able to curate sustainable psycho-social care plans and see the case to completion with an exit strategy
Requirements: click to see more
  1. Masters in Social Work or any other field related to the humanities
  2. At least 2 years of experience in case work dealing with women and children at risk
  3. Well versed with the local protection infrastructures
  4. Strong administrative skills
  5. Able to drive and have own means of transport
  6. Able to communicate in Bahasa Malaysia and English
  7. Able to go the extra mile, have a good attitude and willing to learn

Counsellor / Clinical Psychologist

If you’re that unusual someone who listens deeply before you speak, sees what’s not spoken, knows you’re as fallible as the next person, and will put your heart into helping broken, confused and rejected humanity, apply to join the team.

Key Responsibilities: click to see more
  1. Identify psychological, emotional or behavioral issues
  2. Diagnose psychological, emotional or behavioral disorders
  3. Develop and implement treatment plans and therapeutic processes for the student (client)
  4. Help clients define goals and plan action to achieve personal, social, educational and vocational development and adjustment
  5. Monitor client progress through regular meetings or sessions
  6. Documents therapy sessions in accordance with policies and procedures regarding medical records; implements and adheres to policies which ensure patient confidentiality.
Requirements: click to see more
  1. Must have Masters degree in Counselling/Clinical Psychology or related field
  2. At least 2 years work experience
  3. Possesses relevant license
  4. Enjoys working with children & youths ages 6-18 years
  5. Able to work independently with minimal supervision
  6. Strong interpersonal and communication skills with ability to communicate comfortably with students, parents, staff and community members in a professional manner
  7. Creative, resourceful, self motivated & results oriented
  8. Good interviewing and psychological/developmental evaluation skills
